# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Radia serves three HR/workspace operations audiences: regular employees, workspace admins, and platform/server admins. Employees use it to review their dashboard, complete assigned tasks, read SOPs, understand the org chart, and progress through onboarding. Workspace admins create and manage tasks, people, SOPs, courses, integrations, and workspace preferences. Platform admins monitor system health, access requests, workspaces, and audit activity.

## Product Purpose

Radia is a unified HR management workspace. It gives teams one operational place for task management, organization structure, onboarding, SOP knowledge, integrations, settings, and administration. Success means users can scan the state of the workspace quickly, trust the data, and complete people-ops work without decorative friction.

## Brand Personality

Calm, competent, direct. Radia should feel like trustworthy operations software: modern SaaS, professional but not stiff, and clear enough for repeated daily use. The interface should prioritize confidence, readability, and flow over spectacle.

## Anti-references

Avoid heavy glassmorphism, floating gradient orbs, rainbow-gradient avatars, decorative gradients, emoji in product chrome, hype copy, invented controls, and marketing-style hero layouts inside authenticated surfaces. Do not make Radia feel like a generic AI SaaS template, a playful consumer app, or a dense enterprise-gray back office.

## Design Principles

1. Let the tool disappear into the task.
2. Make hierarchy obvious before adding decoration.
3. Use indigo as a functional accent, not an ornament.
4. Keep role and status cues consistent across the product.
5. Treat admin power with extra clarity, restraint, and recovery paths.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for authenticated product UI. Preserve visible keyboard focus, sufficient contrast, semantic controls, clear error messaging, responsive layouts, and reduced-motion behavior. Do not encode status by color alone.
